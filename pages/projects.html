Research Projects

  1. Automatic Karyotyping of Human chromosomes: The process of karyotyping is usually carried out manually by experts for diagnosis of birth defects or various disorders. We have automated the step of chromosome classification in the karyotyping using Deep neural networks. Due to scarcity of data, we have implemented Siamese Networks (Paper here) which expediate the training and testing process, keeping the accuracy same as that of traditional CNN. In addition, we have also implemented Attention based CNN + LSTM model which boost the accuracy by greater factor. (make changes here after paper submission)
  2. Extracting information from Document Images: In manufacturing units, the inspection of machines is carried out manually by an engineer who marks the fault type and zone using handwritten arrows and text on the standardized inspection sheet. These sheets are then camera scanned and read manually by a team to maintain a log of the faults in their systems, which is quite time consuming. Therefore, we are building a pipeline (Paper here) to automate this step. Our pipeline consists of multiple stages which are implemented using various Computer vision and Deep learning techniques. Currently, our main focus is on the last stage i.e., improving the handwritten text recognition stage from the documents and then pushing this data from inspection sheets to log files.

Projects which formed a part of my graduate requirements:

  1. Shadow Removal and automatic segmentation in OCT images of Optic nerve head: Segmentation of human optic nerve head (ONH) in Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) images becomes difficult when shadow appear due to presence of blood vessels. We aim to improve the quality of OCT images by removal of shadows and then automatically segmenting the retinal layer of interest in Spectral domain OCT using graph theory and dynamic programming. After extraction of choroid layer using segmentation and SSIM techniques, we focused our interest to extract the blood vessels present in it.
